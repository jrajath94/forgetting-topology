\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We mapped the per-component forgetting topology of Qwen3-8B via causal tracing, introduced the Forgetting Vulnerability Score (\fvs{}), and showed that restricting LoRA to the $50\%$ most resistant components (\tglora{}) reduces average prior-task forgetting by \pend{forget_reduction_pct} while matching standard LoRA on downstream tasks. Beyond the PEFT recipe, the topology yields a \textit{mechanistic} account of forgetting: early-layer and anchor-head resistance is consistent with a division between task-agnostic linguistic competence and task-specific key-value storage in late-layer MLPs. This reframes forgetting as a question about \textit{which representational real-estate is being re-used}, and gives practitioners an interpretability-grounded rule for choosing which modules to adapt and which to preserve. Future work will test whether this topology transfers to other 8B-scale architectures.
